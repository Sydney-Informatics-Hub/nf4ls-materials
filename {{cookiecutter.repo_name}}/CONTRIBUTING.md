# Contribution guide

Thank you for your interest in contributing to to the training materials!

These materials are developed collaboratively and we welcome
contributions that improve clarity, pedagogy, accuracy, and
reusability for trainers and facilitators.

## Code of conduct

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inclusive environment
for all contributors and expect all contributors to follow the [CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).

## What kinds of contributions are welcome?

We especially welcome contributions that reflect learner feedback after training delivery such as:

- Improve lesson structure or teaching flow
- Clarify instructor notes or explanations
- Add or refine exercises and examples that support learning objectives
- Fix errors, inconsistencies, or outdated content

Contributions do not need to be large to be valuable!

## How to contribute

1. Open an issue to discuss your idea, or to report a problem.
2. Fork the repository and create a branch for your changes.
3. Submit a pull request describing:
   - What you changed
   - Why the change is helpful for trainers

We encourage submitting draft pull requests to discuss any issues as they come up and to ensure your contribution aligns with the teaching goals, scope of the workshop, and intended audience.

## Review process

All contributions will be reviewed by a maintainer from the Sydney Informatics Hub.

We aim to provide constructive feedback and to respond in a timely
manner, but review timelines may vary depending on availability.

## Attribution and licensing

By contributing, you agree that your contributions will be licensed
under the **Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International (CC BY 4.0)**
licence, consistent with the rest of the repository.

Significant contributions may be acknowledged in the `AUTHORS` file.
Minor contributions are recorded via the GitHub commit history.
